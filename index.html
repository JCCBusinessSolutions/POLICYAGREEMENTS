In short: if a feature that was working at delivery breaks due to an error in JCC's code, that's covered. If it's something the Buyer's setup, sheet, or third-party service is doing, that's outside this warranty.

Part 2: Data Privacy Policy
This policy explains how personal data is handled when an Advisor uses the Client Pulse app, and what role JCC Business Solutions does and does not play in that handling. It is written to align with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173), its Implementing Rules and Regulations, and issuances of the National Privacy Commission.

1. Who This Policy Is For

Client Pulse is a tool built by JCC Business Solutions ("JCC," "we," "us") and licensed to financial advisors ("Advisor," "you") for managing premium due reminders and birthday greetings for the Advisor's own clients. This policy applies to every Advisor who uses Client Pulse, and describes how the personal data of the Advisor's clients ("Client Data") is handled within the app.

2. Roles Under the Data Privacy Act

Under Republic Act No. 10173, there is an important distinction between a Personal Information Controller (the party who decides what personal data to collect and how it will be used) and a Personal Information Processor (a party that processes data on behalf of and under the instructions of a controller). Here is how those roles apply to Client Pulse:

  • The Advisor is the Personal Information Controller. The Advisor decides which clients to add, what client information to store, and how reminders are sent. The Advisor is responsible for obtaining proper consent from their clients and for complying with the Data Privacy Act with respect to their own client relationships.
  • JCC Business Solutions is the software provider, not a controller or processor of Client Data. JCC built and licensed the Client Pulse software, but does not collect, store, view, or process the Advisor's Client Data in the course of the app's normal operation. Section 3 explains why.

3. How Client Pulse Actually Handles Data

This is the part most Advisors want to understand clearly, so here it is in plain terms:

  1. Client Pulse runs as a self-contained web app inside the Advisor's own browser.
  2. When an Advisor sets up Client Pulse, they connect it to their own Google Sheets, which they create and control, using their own Google account.
  3. The Advisor also deploys their own Google Apps Script (a small backend program) under their own Google account. This is what actually stores and retrieves Client Data such as names, policy numbers, premium amounts, due dates, contact numbers, and birthdays.
  4. When the Advisor uses Client Pulse, their browser talks directly to their own Google Apps Script and their own Google Sheets. Client Data flows only between those two points, both of which belong to and are controlled by the Advisor.
The key point: JCC Business Solutions does not own, host, or operate any server that Client Data passes through. JCC has no login access, no viewing access, and no copy of the Advisor's Google Sheets or Apps Script deployment. Client names, policy numbers, premium amounts due, contact numbers, email addresses, and birthdates stay entirely within the Advisor's own Google account unless the Advisor chooses to share that information with someone else.

4. What JCC Does Not Collect or Access

In the normal operation of Client Pulse, JCC and its developers do not collect, receive, view, or store any of the following:

  • Client names
  • Policy numbers
  • Premium or amount due figures
  • Contact numbers, email addresses, or home addresses of the Advisor's clients
  • Birthdates or any other personal information belonging to the Advisor's clients

None of this information passes through, is logged by, or is stored on any system operated by JCC.

5. What JCC May Collect

JCC's collection of personal data is limited to information about the Advisor as a buyer and user of the software, such as:

  • The Advisor's own name, email address, and contact details, used for delivering the app, invoicing, and providing support;
  • Payment confirmation details needed to process a purchase, such as a reference number, which the Advisor provides directly;
  • Communications the Advisor sends to JCC, such as support requests or feedback.

6. Technical Support and Incidental Access

If an Advisor voluntarily shares information with JCC to help diagnose a technical issue, for example a screenshot, a sample data export, or a description that includes a client's name, JCC may incidentally come across limited Client Data during that support interaction. In such cases, JCC commits to the following:

  • The information will be used only to diagnose and resolve the specific issue raised;
  • The information will not be sold, shared with, or disclosed to any third party;
  • The information will not be retained longer than necessary to resolve the issue, and will be deleted upon request once the issue is resolved;
  • JCC will not use such information for marketing, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to the support request.

Advisors are encouraged to remove or mask sensitive client details (such as full policy numbers) when sharing screenshots for support whenever practical.

7. Data Security

Because Client Data lives in the Advisor's own Google Sheets and Google Apps Script deployment, the security of that data depends primarily on how the Advisor manages their own Google account, including password strength, two-factor authentication, and sheet sharing permissions. JCC follows reasonable secure coding practices in building Client Pulse, such as requiring HTTPS connections and email-based access gating, but cannot guarantee the security of the Advisor's own Google Workspace environment, device, or network.

8. Rights of the Advisor's Clients

Because the Advisor is the Personal Information Controller for their own clients, it is the Advisor's responsibility, not JCC's, to:

  • Obtain proper consent from clients before adding their information to Client Pulse or sending them automated reminders;
  • Honor client requests related to their rights under the Data Privacy Act, including the right to be informed, the right to access, the right to correct, the right to object, and the right to erasure or blocking;
  • Respond to any privacy complaint or inquiry a client raises about how their data is being used.

Since JCC has no access to the underlying Client Data, JCC is not in a position to fulfill these requests directly. Any such request should be directed to the Advisor who manages that client's information.

9. Data Retention

JCC does not retain Client Data, since it does not receive or store it in the first place. How long Client Data is kept within the Advisor's own Google Sheets is governed entirely by the Advisor's own retention practices and policies.

10. Third-Party Services Involved

Client Pulse relies on a small number of third-party platforms to function. These are services the Advisor sets up and controls under their own account, not services operated by JCC:

  • Google Sheets and Google Apps Script, under the Advisor's own Google account, used to store and process Client Data;
  • GitHub Pages or an equivalent static hosting service, used only to serve the Client Pulse app file itself, which does not store or process Client Data;
  • If the Advisor's setup uses any email or messaging service to deliver notifications or access codes, that service may process limited contact information, such as an email address, solely to deliver that message.

Because Google's infrastructure operates globally, Client Data stored in the Advisor's Google Sheets may be processed or stored on servers located outside the Philippines, as part of Google's standard service infrastructure. This is a function of the Advisor's choice to use Google Workspace, not something JCC controls.

11. Children's Data

Client Pulse is intended for managing the Advisor's adult clients in connection with financial products and services. It is not intended for the collection of personal data belonging to minors, and Advisors should avoid entering such data into the app.
